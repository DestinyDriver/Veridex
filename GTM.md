# Go-to-Market

## 1. Exact target user

**Engineering Chief of Staff / Head of DevEx / Eng Ops Manager at a Series A or B startup, 30–150 engineers.** Specifically: someone who got handed renewal authority for dev tooling six months ago, owns the Cursor + Copilot + Claude + ChatGPT Teams + Vercel bills, but has no procurement team behind them. They sit between a CTO who doesn't want to think about it and a CFO who just flagged "AI tools" as a line item in last week's board deck. Not VPs of Eng (too senior, won't self-serve). Not solo founders (too small to matter).

## 2. What they Google right before needing this

- "cursor ultra vs pro for teams"
- "github copilot business vs enterprise worth it"
- "how to consolidate ai subscriptions"
- "anthropic teams vs api pricing"
- "ai tools renewal negotiation"
- Screenshot threads on X: "our monthly AI bill is now bigger than AWS lol"

## 3. Where they hang out

- **Rands Leadership Slack** (#tools, #engops) — the single highest concentration of this exact persona online
- **LeadDev Slack** + LeadDev conference attendee list
- **r/ExperiencedDevs** and **r/EngineeringManagers**
- **DX Slack** (Abi Noda's community, ~5k devex/eng-ops people)
- **Lenny's Slack** (#tools-and-stack channel)
- **Pragmatic Engineer paid Discord** — Gergely's audience overlaps almost perfectly
- X lists curated by Will Larson, Charity Majors, Camille Fournier

## 4. First 100 users in 30 days, $0

1. **"I audited 20 YC W24 companies' AI bills" public teardown** — anonymised, on X + HN + a Substack post. The data becomes the lead magnet.
2. **Free audits for 10 named-but-anonymised startups in exchange for a one-line quote.** Use the quotes on the landing page.
3. **Rands #tools channel post** — not a pitch, a "here's what I learned auditing 20 stacks" share. Veridex link goes in the footer.
4. **Show HN with a specific savings number in the title** ("Show HN: a tool that found us $1,840/mo in duplicate AI subs").
5. **Cold LinkedIn DMs to 50 Eng Ops / DevEx titles at Series A/B companies** — offer to audit their stack live on a 15-min call.
6. **Get listed in Lenny's Tools roundup** — one email, two-line pitch.
7. **One eng leadership podcast guest spot** (Engineering Enablement, Dev Interrupted) — they need guests every week.

## 5. The unfair channel

**Publish a public "AI Tooling Price Index" updated monthly** — every tool, every plan, every recent price change, with an opinionated "who should buy this" column. Nobody else has the engine to keep this current at scale. It becomes the canonical reference link in every "how should I price our AI tools" Slack thread. Bonus if Credex's vendor relationships unlock **audited-only discounts** (e.g. 15% off Cursor team plans for Veridex audit completers) — that's a benefit literally no competitor can replicate.

## 6. Week-1 traction if this works

- **50 audits completed**, 15 with email captured
- **1 Show HN reaching front page** (≥80 points) **or** one X thread hitting 100k impressions
- **3 inbound consultation requests** from the in-product CTA
- **5 audit reports shared publicly** via the short-link system (measurable via `share_links` clicks)
- **1 inbound from a Rands Slack member** asking to audit their company
- **$0 spent, ≥3 distinct acquisition channels validated**

If after week 1 only one channel is working, kill the rest and concentrate. If none are working, the persona is wrong — not the product.
